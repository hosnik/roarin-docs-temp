# The Den

The Den is where the real action happens. It's a building in the game world — walk in, and you're in your deployment hub. Dark room, monitor glows, operations room energy. This is where your monsters go from fighters to traders.

---

## What's Inside

Four stations, each serving a different function in your trading operation:

### Deploy Desk

*"Ready to send one of yours out?"*

Select a monster from your party. See its traits, market affinity, and effective trading configuration previewed in real-time. Allocate paper USDC from your available balance. Confirm deployment.

Your monster is now trading autonomously on real prediction market data.

### The Board

A wall-mounted display showing everything happening with your deployed monsters:

- Active deployments and current P&L
- Live positions and recent trades
- Performance history
- Portfolio overview

**Available balance:** What you can spend on items or new deployments
**Deployed capital:** What's currently allocated to trading monsters
**Total portfolio:** Everything combined

### Recall Handler

*"Want to pull someone back?"*

View your deployed monsters' performance. Hit recall when you want them back in your party. The system closes their open positions — you'll see a "wrapping up trades..." state while positions settle.

This isn't instant. Your monster committed capital to real positions. Settling takes a moment. That anticipation is part of the experience.

### Market Ticker

A wall-mounted ticker showing trending prediction markets — current odds, opportunities, and edges filtered by your monsters' affinities. Browse what's out there before deciding who to deploy and where.

## Capital Allocation

When you deploy a monster, you choose how much to stake:

1. Check your available balance (say $1,000 paper USDC)
2. Select a monster from your party
3. Allocate an amount (say $300)
4. That $300 moves from your available balance to the monster's trading account
5. Monster trades against its own $300, not your full balance

**On recall:** Whatever's left (original + P&L) returns to your available balance.

**Why allocation?** If multiple monsters shared one balance, a losing monster would drain capital from winners. Allocation teaches capital management — a real trading concept — and isolates risk between your deployed agents.

**Example portfolio view:**
- Available: $700
- Deployed: Holdox ($320, +$20 P&L) | Ripjaw ($280, -$20 P&L)
- Total: $1,300

## Deployment Limits

Your player tier determines how many monsters can be deployed at once:

| Tier | Rating | Simultaneous Deployments |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **C** (Starting) | — | 1 monster |
| **B** | 1200+ | 2 monsters |
| **A** | 1450+ | 3 monsters |
| **S** | 1700+ | 4 monsters |

Rating improves through battle wins and deployment performance. Better tier = more deployed monsters = more diversified trading = more potential returns.
