# Battle System

Battles are where your monsters prove themselves. Every fight sharpens their edge, earns XP, and teaches you how they think — which matters when you deploy them to markets.

---

## How Battles Work

Wild monsters appear in encounter zones. NPC trainers challenge you on sight. Either way, it's your monster's moves against theirs.

**Phase 1 (Current):** You select moves directly from an expanded pool of 10-15 per species. Four moves equipped at a time, swappable from your party menu. This is classic monster-battler gameplay — pick your attack, read the situation, adapt.

**Phase 2 (Coming):** Coaching postures replace direct move selection. Instead of picking specific attacks, you set a battle posture and your monster chooses its own move based on its personality traits and the situation.

---

## Coaching Postures (Phase 2)

You're not controlling the monster — you're coaching it.

| Posture | What Your Monster Does |
| --- | --- |
| **Aggro** | Favors highest-damage moves. High risk, high reward. |
| **Steady** | Plays balanced — mixes offense and defense based on the situation. |
| **Guard** | Favors defensive and healing moves. Trades damage output for survival. |
| **Wildcard** | Ignores your coaching entirely. Picks based purely on personality traits. Unpredictable. |

The same posture produces different results with different monsters. A Holdox on Aggro calculates its big swing. A Ripjaw on Aggro just starts swinging. You learn their tendencies through experience — which is exactly what happens when you deploy them to markets.

**Two different monsters given the same posture make different choices.** The player learns their monster's personality through battle, mirroring learning how their agent behaves in different market conditions.

## Moves

Each species has 8-12 unique moves. Monsters learn new moves as they level up. Four can be equipped at once — choose based on your battle strategy or upcoming deployment needs.

---

## Battle Rewards

- **XP** to all non-fainted party monsters (active get 100%, backline get 50%)
- **Paper USDC** — 10-30 for wild battles, 50-200 for trainer battles
- **Items** — random drops from trainer battles

No loss penalty in beta. You faint, you heal up, you get back out there.

---

## The Connection to Trading

Battle isn't separate from the trading game. It IS the trading game:

- A monster that fights aggressively in battle → trades aggressively in markets
- Learning your monster's tendencies in combat → understanding how it'll behave when deployed
- Leveling through battle → directly improves trading capacity (higher bet limits, more capabilities)
- Building a diverse battle team → building a diversified trading portfolio

The game teaches you trading concepts through play. You never have to read a whitepaper.
