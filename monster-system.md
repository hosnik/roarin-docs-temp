# Monster System

Five species. Each one wired different. Each one trades different.

Every monster in Roarin is backed by a real trading agent. Its species determines what markets it can trade. Its traits determine how it trades. No two monsters are the same — even within the same species.

---

## The Five Species

| Species | Market Affinity | Personality | How It Trades |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Holdox** | Basketball / Football | The Strategist | Patient, high-conviction. Waits for clear edge before committing. Goes big when it sees the play. |
| **Ripjaw** | Combat / MMA | The Maverick | Aggressive, high-frequency. Takes smaller edges but trades often. Never sits still. |
| **Median** | Baseball / Hockey | The All-Rounder | Balanced risk, balanced activity. Won't make you rich overnight, won't blow up your account either. |
| **Shellguard** | Politics / Events | The Guardian | Conservative, capital-preservation focused. Small position sizes, tight stops. Your safe play. |
| **Slashburn** | Crypto / Speculative | The Shark | Momentum-following. Rides trends, cuts losers fast. High risk, high reward. |

### Starter Choice

When you start the game, Rex offers you three:

- **Holdox** — "Patient. Waits for the right read, then commits hard."
- **Ripjaw** — "Aggressive. Swings at everything. Wins big, loses big. Never boring."
- **Median** — "Balanced. Steady hand. Won't blow up your account."

Shellguard and Slashburn? You'll have to find those in the wild.

## Market Affinity

Market affinity defines which prediction markets a monster can be deployed to. Each species has a primary affinity — the markets where it performs best.

As monsters level up, they unlock secondary affinities. A level 1 Holdox can only trade NBA/NFL. A level 20 Holdox might unlock politics markets — with reduced edge sensitivity compared to its primary domain, but still functional.

**Adding new species = unlocking new markets.** A future monster covering soccer opens EPL, La Liga, MLS. New zones contain new species that access new verticals. Content expansion and market expansion are the same thing.

## Traits

Every individual monster has four core traits, rolled at catch time. These are permanent and determine its specific trading behavior.

| Trait | Low (1-3) | High (8-10) | What It Controls |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Conviction** | Hedges frequently, small positions | Goes big on high-confidence plays | Position sizing |
| **Patience** | Trades on small edges (3%+) | Only trades on large edges (8%+) | Edge threshold |
| **Resilience** | Tight stop-losses, exits early | Wide stops, rides through volatility | Stop-loss width |
| **Focus** | Spreads across many markets | Concentrates on fewer positions | Max open positions |

### Species Trait Means

Each species has a natural tendency. Most Holdox are patient and high-conviction — but you might catch one with Patience 4. That's a rare aggressive variant. Worth keeping.

| Species | Conviction | Patience | Resilience | Focus |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Holdox | 6 | 7 | 5 | 6 |
| Ripjaw | 7 | 3 | 4 | 3 |
| Median | 5 | 5 | 5 | 5 |
| Shellguard | 3 | 6 | 7 | 7 |
| Slashburn | 8 | 4 | 3 | 4 |

Traits are hidden during wild encounters. You see species and level only. Traits reveal after catching — creating a gambling element every time you throw a ball. "Let me see what I got."

## Leveling & Progression

Monsters level through battle XP and deployment experience.

- **XP curve:** Level cubed. Early levels are fast. Late levels are a grind.
- **Stat gains:** HP and Attack increase each level with some randomness
- **Move learning:** New moves unlock at specific level thresholds (8-12 moves per species, 4 equipped at a time)
- **Affinity expansion:** Level 10 and 20 unlock secondary market affinities
- **Trait sharpening:** Every 5 levels, one random trait increases by 1 (capped at 10). Your monsters get more extreme over time.

## Party & Collection

- **Active party:** Up to 6 monsters
- **Collection:** Unlimited storage for monsters not in your party
- **Deployed monsters leave your party.** You can't battle with a monster that's currently trading. Your best fighter might be your best earner — choose wisely.

Future: Monster trading between players. Because every monster is backed by a unique agent record, ownership transfer is built into the architecture.
